About

I am called to the intersection of service and science that medicine embodies. I am currently pursuing my pre-medical coursework at The George Washington University School of Medicine and Health Sciences.

I previously worked as a post-bacc researcher in Dr. Ev Fedorenko's language lab (EvLab) at MIT. There, I also served as the project coordinator of Interesting Brains, a research initiative investigating the functional topography of the cortex in individuals with substantial brain lesions (e.g., arachnoid cysts, surgical resections, hydrocephalus).

I am originally from Memphis, Tennessee, and enjoy reading and writing fiction in my free time. Though I was formerly (and perennially) a barista, I have yet to perfect latte art.
